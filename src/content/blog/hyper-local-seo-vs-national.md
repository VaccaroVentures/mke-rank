Most “SEO best practices” articles assume ecommerce or SaaS. Trades are different: the buyer is urgent, the decision window is short, and proof lives in your Google Business Profile, your reviews, and the neighborhoods you actually serve.

National playbooks over-index on blog volume and under-index on geography. For HVAC or plumbing in the Milwaukee metro, a handful of well-structured service-area pages plus a disciplined GBP beats fifty generic posts titled “5 tips to save energy.”

**What hyper-local means in practice**

- **Zip and neighborhood intent** — People search “plumber Bay View” or “AC repair Brookfield,” not just city names.
- **Drive-time truth** — Your service area should match dispatch reality, not a perfect circle.
- **Proof tied to job types** — Photos and reviews that mention the actual work (tankless install, sewer scope) outperform vague five-star praise.

**Where AI helps (without cutting corners)**

Use AI to draft faster, not to fake expertise. First drafts for FAQs, Q&A, and seasonal GBP posts are fair game—your licensed team should still verify anything touching codes, safety, or warranties.

If your agency cannot explain how they handle Google’s guidelines on reviews and doorway pages, keep shopping. Local SEO for trades is part strategy, part operator discipline—and it should sound like your crew, not a content farm.
