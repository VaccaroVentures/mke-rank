Reviews influence clicks, conversions, and how confident a homeowner feels before they call. The goal is steady velocity—not a one-time spike that looks suspicious.

**Timing beats bribery**

Ask after success moments: post-install walkthrough, first mow of the season, cleared drain, completed restoration dry-out—when satisfaction is highest.

**Segment by job type**

Roofing reviews should mention project scale. Emergency plumbing should mention speed and communication. Mixed proof helps future customers see themselves.

**Reply with specifics**

Thank the customer, reference the job where appropriate, and invite next steps (“Call us any time for…” ). AI drafts can accelerate replies, but the best responses feel human and accountable.

**Avoid**

- Gatekeeping reviews (“only if you had a good experience” written the wrong way).
- Incentives that violate platform rules.
- Copy-paste responses that ignore what the customer actually said.

Sustainable reputation tracks with operational excellence. Marketing can amplify—but it cannot fake—how you show up on the truck roll.
